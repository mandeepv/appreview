# Privacy Policy for Kinderwell

**Last Updated:** July 4, 2026

## Introduction

Welcome to Kinderwell. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use our mobile application.

## Information We Collect

### 1. Account Information

When you sign up for Kinderwell, we collect:

- **Email address** (from Google or Apple Sign-In)
- **Name** (from Google or Apple Sign-In — Apple only provides your name on the very first authorization)
- **User ID** (generated by our authentication provider)

### 2. Onboarding & Profile Information

During onboarding you tell us about your parenting context so the app can personalize lessons. This is stored on our servers so you don't lose it if you reinstall the app:

- **Your parent type** (mother / father / other)
- **Your age**
- **Your children's information** — number of children, their age ranges, and their gender
- **Your parenting goals, styles you're familiar with, and emotional challenges you'd like help with**
- **Whether your partner is involved in parenting**
- **Whether you've enabled notifications**

We do NOT collect your children's names or exact birthdates.

### 3. Usage Data

We collect information about how you use the app:

- **Lesson progress** (which lessons you've started and completed)
- **Screen navigation** (which screens you view and in what order)
- **Interaction events** (e.g., when you tap "Continue" in onboarding, when a paywall is shown, when you tap a plan)
- **App lifecycle events** (installed, opened, backgrounded)
- **Device information** (device model, OS version, app version, locale, timezone)

### 4. Subscription Data

We collect information related to your subscription:

- **Subscription status** (active / inactive)
- **Product identifier** for the plan you selected
- **Whether a purchase attempt was started, completed, abandoned, or failed**
- **Payment information is handled entirely by Apple and is NOT accessible to us.** Your credit card number is never seen by Kinderwell.

### 5. Automatically Collected Data

- **Device identifiers** (for analytics — anonymized where possible)
- **IP address** (transient — used by our infrastructure for security; not stored long-term against your account)
- **Crash logs and diagnostic information** (to detect and fix bugs)

### 6. What We Do NOT Collect

- We do NOT collect your precise geographic location.
- We do NOT collect microphone, camera, contacts, photos, or health data.
- We do NOT track you across other companies' apps or websites for advertising.
- We do NOT sell your personal data to anyone.

## How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

1. **Provide the Service**
   - Authenticate your account
   - Save and sync your lesson progress and onboarding answers across devices
   - Deliver personalized parenting content based on your family's context

2. **Improve the App**
   - Analyze usage patterns (via PostHog) to understand where users drop off, which content works, and how to improve
   - Detect and fix crashes and errors (via Sentry)
   - A/B test alternative onboarding flows to make the experience better
   - Develop new content based on aggregate user needs

3. **Communicate with You**
   - Send important service updates
   - Respond to support requests
   - Notify you about subscription-related changes (via Apple)

4. **Legal Compliance**
   - Comply with legal obligations
   - Enforce our Terms of Service
   - Protect against fraud and abuse

## Data Storage and Security

### Where We Store Your Data

Your data is stored securely using:

- **Supabase** (cloud database provider, hosted on AWS in the United States)
- **Encryption:** All data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256)

### Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures:

- Encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS)
- Secure authentication protocols (OAuth 2.0)
- Row-Level Security (RLS) at the database layer so users can only access their own records
- Access controls and monitoring
- Automatic logout after extended inactivity

### Data Retention

- **Active accounts:** We retain your data as long as your account is active.
- **Deleted accounts:** All personal data is permanently deleted within 30 days of account deletion. Deletion cascades across authentication, profile, and lesson progress records.
- **Legal requirements:** We may retain certain data if required by law.

## Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services. Each has their own privacy policy governing how they handle your data.

### 1. Authentication Providers

- **Google Sign-In** ([Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)) — used if you sign in with your Google account
- **Apple Sign-In** ([Apple Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/)) — used if you sign in with your Apple ID

### 2. Database and Backend

- **Supabase** ([Supabase Privacy Policy](https://supabase.com/privacy)) — stores user accounts, onboarding answers, and lesson progress. Hosted on AWS in the United States.

### 3. Payment Processing

- **Apple In-App Purchase** ([Apple Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/)) — handles all subscription payments. We never see or store your payment information.

### 4. Subscription Management

- **Superwall** ([Superwall Privacy Policy](https://superwall.com/privacy)) — displays the paywall and reports whether you have an active subscription. Superwall receives a hashed device identifier and your product-selection events, but never sees your name, email, or Kinderwell profile data.

### 5. Product Analytics

- **PostHog** ([PostHog Privacy Policy](https://posthog.com/privacy)) — receives events describing which screens you view, which onboarding options you select, whether you complete lessons, and paywall interactions. We identify you to PostHog using your Kinderwell user ID and email so we can understand user journeys. PostHog stores this data in the United States. **We use PostHog data only in aggregate, and only to improve the app.** PostHog is not used to build advertising profiles about you.

### 6. Crash Reporting

- **Sentry** ([Sentry Privacy Policy](https://sentry.io/privacy/)) — receives stack traces and app state when the app crashes or hits a handled error. Sentry does NOT receive your name, email, or profile answers. It receives device metadata (OS version, app version, device model) and error context.

## Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your data:

### 1. Access Your Data

You can view your account information in the Settings screen of the app. To request a full export, email us.

### 2. Correct Your Data

Contact us at kinderwellteam@gmail.com to update any incorrect information.

### 3. Delete Your Data

You can delete your account at any time:

Deletion cascades — your authentication record, profile, onboarding answers, and lesson progress are all removed. Analytics events already collected in PostHog and error records in Sentry may persist for a limited period in aggregate but are no longer linked to you.

Contact us at kinderwellteam@gmail.com to request a copy of your data.

### 5. Opt-Out of Communications

You can opt out of push notifications through your device settings.

### 6. Withdraw Consent

You can withdraw consent for data processing by deleting your account.

## Children's Privacy

Kinderwell is designed for parents and caregivers — its users must be 18 or older. **The app is not intended for use by children, and we do not knowingly collect any personal information directly from children under 13.** If we discover we have collected such information from a child under 13, we will delete it immediately.

However, because Kinderwell is a parenting app, the parent using the app will tell us about their child or children in order to personalize lessons. Specifically, during onboarding a parent may tell us about their children:

- **Number of children**
- **Age range** (e.g. "0–1", "2–4", "5–7") — not exact birthdates
- **Gender** (or "prefer not to say")

This information IS transmitted to our servers (Supabase) and stored in the parent's account so that lesson personalization survives reinstall or a device change. Age range is also sent to PostHog as an anonymized user property so we can understand which cohorts benefit most from the app and improve the experience for each.

We do NOT collect your children's names, exact birthdates, photos, voice recordings, contact information, or any other directly identifying details. We do NOT communicate directly with your children.

The parent providing this information has full control:

- They can update it any time within the app
- They can delete it entirely via **Settings → Delete Account**
- They may choose to skip questions during onboarding
